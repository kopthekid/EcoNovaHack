Five Core Themes and Guiding Questions

1) Sustainable Energy and Decarbonization

Solutions to accelerate renewable energy adoption, improve energy efficiency, and reduce carbon footprints.

Guiding Question: How can we build technology that helps individuals, communities, and small businesses track energy use and carbon footprint in real time, then offer personalized, actionable recommendations?

2) Circular Economy and Waste Reduction

Tools to eliminate waste, keep materials in use, and improve systems for sharing, repair, and recycling.

Guiding Question: How can we create tools that help people and businesses reduce waste by enabling repair, sharing/renting, and clear local recycling/composting guidance for complex materials?

3) Sustainable Food and Agriculture Systems

Web platforms that connect consumers to sustainable food sources, reduce food waste, and support regenerative farming.

Guiding Question: What digital systems can build a more resilient food network, from direct farmer connections to surplus food redistribution that cuts waste and food miles?

4) Biodiversity, Conservation, and Community Engagement

Solutions to protect ecosystems, engage public participation, and strengthen community connection with nature.

Guiding Question: How might we design technology that enables communities to monitor local air/water quality and biodiversity, report environmental hazards, and join citizen science restoration efforts?

5) Data-Driven Sustainability and Resource Planning

Use of geospatial, IoT, and demographic data to model impacts, optimize resources, and support policy/advocacy decisions.

Guiding Question: How can we use geospatial data, satellite imagery, and AI to model climate risks, identify optimal locations for green infrastructure, and guide urban sustainability planning?
